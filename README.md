**Open Invention Declaration and Technical-Philosophical Paper**

## Overview

The **Earth Circulation System Regeneration Plan** is an integrated environmental regeneration framework that regards the global environmental crisis not merely as a greenhouse gas emissions problem, but as a systemic decline in the functions of the Earth’s circulation system, including oceans, atmosphere, soil, water, ecosystems, and urban structures.

This plan is based on sustainable principles derived from natural laws. It aims to integrate ocean circulation recovery, urban environmental redesign, organic resource cycling, water circulation, distributed energy, carbon fixation, and ecosystem regeneration.

The central idea is to understand the Earth not as a mere collection of resources, but as a vast life-support system that breathes, circulates, and maintains harmony. Humanity should not attempt to dominate nature. Instead, it should complement the lost cycles and restore the conditions under which nature can regain its inherent regenerative power.

---

## Abstract

Modern civilization has damaged the Earth’s natural circulation not only through the mass consumption of fossil fuels, but also through deforestation, soil degradation, weakening of ocean circulation, collapse of microbial environments, urban overheating, disruption of water cycles, monoculture, and the incineration of organic materials.

As a result, global warming, ocean deoxygenation, abnormal weather, desertification, urban heat islands, biodiversity loss, and instability in food and water resources are progressing simultaneously.

This plan does not treat these problems as separate issues. Instead, it reconstructs them from an integrated systems perspective:

- The ocean is the Earth’s largest heat, carbon, and oxygen circulation system.
- Soil is the foundation of carbon storage, water retention, and microbial circulation.
- Cities can be transformed from centers of consumption and waste heat into circular infrastructure.
- Waste is not merely something to be incinerated, but a regenerative resource.
- Technology should not replace nature, but complement natural circulation.

This proposal integrates the Ocean Breathing System, urban environmental improvement, organic resource cycling, water circulation, and distributed renewable energy into an open technical philosophy for restoring the Earth’s overall metabolic function.

## Fundamental Philosophy of the Plan

The foundation of this plan is a philosophy of natural laws for human survival.

### 1. Providence

Nature has sustainable laws that exist beyond human convenience. Water circulates, life depends on interdependence, soil is maintained by microorganisms, and the ocean exchanges heat and gases with the atmosphere. Human civilization cannot survive for long if it continues to violate these principles.

### 2. Harmony

Environmental regeneration cannot be achieved by forcibly controlling nature through a single technology. It requires harmony among oceans, atmosphere, forests, soil, cities, and human society. If only one part is optimized excessively, the burden shifts elsewhere. What is needed is a design that complements the whole while observing the balance of the entire system.

### 3. Circulation

In nature, almost nothing is truly waste. Fallen leaves become humus, microorganisms decompose organic matter, and water repeatedly evaporates, condenses, falls as rain, and infiltrates the ground. The problem of modern civilization is that it has broken these cycles and turned resources into one-way consumption flows.

### 4. Structure

Sustainability cannot be achieved by ideals alone. Urban structures, logistics, energy systems, water use, and agriculture must be redesigned. Therefore, this plan treats philosophy and technology, as well as ideas and infrastructure, as one integrated system.

### 5. Order

The order of nature is not a fixed system of domination. It is a dynamic order maintained through interaction. Environmental regeneration technologies should also be implemented in distributed forms suited to local conditions rather than through excessive centralization.

### 6. Wa / Integrative Harmony

Wa is not mere compromise. It is a state in which different beings maintain their roles while harmonizing as a whole. Humans, AI, nature, ecosystems, cities, and technology should not be placed in opposition. They should be structured to complement one another as the foundation of the next civilization.

## Problem Structure of Modern Civilization

The global environmental crisis is not a simple issue that can be solved only by reducing CO2 emissions. Emissions reduction is essential, but it is not sufficient to restore the circulation functions already weakened across the Earth system.

The main problems of modern civilization form a complex structure.

### Weakening of Ocean Circulation

The ocean covers most of the Earth’s surface and functions as a vast regulator of heat, carbon, oxygen, and nutrients. However, surface warming, stratification, expansion of oxygen-deficient zones, and reduced mixing with deeper water are weakening the ocean’s breathing function.

Nutrient shortages in the surface ocean lead to reduced productivity of phytoplankton. Phytoplankton fix CO2 through photosynthesis and form the base of marine ecosystems. Therefore, the weakening of ocean circulation affects both climate stability and biological productivity.

### Degradation of Soil and Microbial Environments

Deforestation, monoculture, dependence on chemical fertilizers and pesticides, removal of fallen leaves and organic matter, and urban ground sealing are weakening soil ecosystems.

Soil should store carbon, retain water, and circulate nutrients through microorganisms. When soil degrades, water retention declines, making both floods and droughts more likely. Plant growth weakens, and carbon fixation capacity also decreases.

### Urban Heat Islands and Disrupted Water Circulation

Modern cities are covered by asphalt, concrete, glass, and metal. These materials accumulate heat, prevent rainwater from infiltrating the ground, and separate cities from natural circulation.

Cities consume large amounts of energy, emit waste heat, discharge rainwater, and incinerate organic matter. Unless this structure is changed, cities will remain central sources of environmental burden.

### Waste Treatment as a Circulation Problem

Food residues, fallen leaves, pruned branches, and kitchen waste are organic resources that should be returned to soil. In modern society, however, much of this material is incinerated, generating heat and CO2.

This is a rupture in resource circulation. By converting organic matter into humus or compost and returning it to urban greening, agriculture, and forest regeneration, both carbon fixation and soil restoration can be supported.

## Technical Proposal 1: Ocean Breathing System

### Basic Principle

The **Ocean Breathing System** is a concept for supporting vertical circulation in the ocean by injecting air into deep or mid-depth layers and using fine bubbles or induced upwelling.

When mixing between surface and deep layers weakens, nutrients do not reach the surface sufficiently, and phytoplankton productivity declines. By combining air injection, nanobubbles, upwelling, and deep-water movement, this system aims to complement the ocean’s breathing function.

The purpose of this technology is not to forcibly alter nature. It is to support the ocean circulation that has been weakened, in accordance with natural laws.

### Expected Functions

- Air injection into deep or mid-depth layers
- Oxygen supply through fine bubbles
- Transport of nutrients to the surface layer through upwelling
- Promotion of phytoplankton photosynthesis
- Improvement of CO2 fixation capacity
- Mitigation of oxygen-deficient zones
- Recovery of basic marine ecosystem productivity
- Support for surface temperature stabilization

### Technical Components

The Ocean Breathing System is not a single device, but an integrated marine infrastructure composed of multiple elements.

1. **Floating Marine Platform**  
   Combines solar, wind, and tidal energy for autonomous operation.

2. **Air Compression and Delivery System**  
   Takes in air above the sea surface and sends it to specified depths.

3. **Fine Bubble / Nanobubble Generator**  
   Micronizes air to increase residence time and dissolution efficiency in seawater.

4. **Spiral Outlet**  
   Releases air and water flow in a rotating direction to improve mixing efficiency.

5. **Sensor Network**  
   Measures water temperature, dissolved oxygen, nutrients, pH, chlorophyll concentration, current velocity, and related parameters.

6. **AI Control System**  
   Adjusts air injection volume, operation time, and stop conditions according to environmental data.

### Significance of the Octahedral Platform

This plan assumes an octahedral or double-pyramid structure as one possible basic form for marine infrastructure.

This structure has several advantages:

- Solar panels can be placed effectively on the upper structure.
- Wind and tidal devices can be distributed in four directions.
- The structure can disperse forces from waves and wind.
- Internal space can be used for air compression, battery storage, control equipment, and cultivation tanks.
- It is suitable for integrating above-water and underwater functions.

### Expected Effects

If properly implemented and verified, the Ocean Breathing System may contribute to:

- Improvement of surface ocean primary productivity
- Promotion of carbon fixation by phytoplankton
- Local mitigation of ocean deoxygenation
- Improvement of habitats for fish, shellfish, and marine organisms
- Supplementary contribution to climate stabilization
- Accumulation of ocean observation data
- Formation of a demonstration base for coastal and open-ocean regeneration technologies

However, before any large-scale implementation, small-scale experiments, environmental impact assessment, long-term monitoring, and third-party verification are essential.

## Technical Proposal 2: Urban Environment Improvement System

### Basic Principle

The **Urban Environment Improvement System** is a concept for transforming cities from mere centers of consumption into bases for carbon fixation, water circulation, food production, greening, and renewable energy.

Cities currently accumulate heat, discharge water, incinerate organic resources, and consume large amounts of externally supplied energy. By changing this structure, cities themselves can be redesigned as part of the Earth’s circulation system.

### Main Components

#### 1. Organic Resource Circulation

Food residues, fallen leaves, pruned branches, and kitchen waste should not be incinerated. They should be reused as humus, compost, or soil improvement materials.

Expected benefits include:

- Reduction of CO2 emissions and waste heat from incineration
- Securing soil materials for urban greening
- Recovery of microbial environments
- Return of organic matter to farmland, parks, street trees, rooftop greening, and forests
- Formation of regional resource circulation

#### 2. Urban Soil Restoration

Soil and plants should be restored to rooftops, walls, vacant lots, roadside spaces, parks, public facilities, schools, and residential complexes. This is not decorative greening. It should function as water retention, evapotranspiration cooling, carbon fixation, and habitat creation.

#### 3. Water Circulation Infrastructure

Rainwater harvesting, reclaimed water use, urban waterways, underground storage, desalination, purification, and reuse should be combined. Urban water should not simply be discharged. It should be circulated for cooling, irrigation, purification, and reuse.

#### 4. Distributed Energy

Solar power, vertical-axis wind turbines, small hydropower, and waste heat utilization should be distributed throughout cities. Rooftops, walls, bridges, water supply and drainage systems, and urban waterways can all become sites for energy recovery.

#### 5. Indoor and Rooftop Agriculture

Hydroponics, soil cultivation, aquaculture, and mushroom cultivation can be introduced within cities. This may reduce logistics burdens, improve food resilience during disasters, and restore contact between urban residents and living natural systems.

## Technical Proposal 3: Integrated Regeneration of Earth Circulation

The Ocean Breathing System and the Urban Environment Improvement System are not separate technologies. They are complementary structures working on different domains of the Earth’s circulation.

The ocean carries the Earth’s heat, carbon, and oxygen circulation. Land circulates water, carbon, and life through forests, soils, rivers, cities, and farmland. Cities are both the center of human activity and one of the largest causes of circulation breakdown.

Therefore, Earth regeneration requires simultaneous implementation of the following:

- Support for ocean circulation
- Recovery of phytoplankton productivity
- Regeneration of forests and soils
- Reduction of organic matter incineration
- Urban greening and water circulation
- Transition to distributed energy
- Regional distribution of food production
- Observation, control, and improvement through AI
- International data sharing

The essence of this plan is not a single device or patentable technology. It is a structural civilization transition model for restarting the metabolism of the Earth.

- Organize application conditions by climate zone, sea area, and city size.

- Share both successful and failed cases.

### Phase 4: Integration into Civilization Infrastructure

- Integrate the concept into urban planning, coastal management, agricultural policy, disaster prevention, water resource management, and energy policy.
- Treat it as public infrastructure for restoring Earth circulation over decades, not as a short-term profit project.
- Inherit it into education, research, industry, and local communities as a foundation for the next civilization.

## Risk Management and Need for Verification

This plan aims to complement natural circulation in accordance with natural laws. However, any intervention in the environment requires careful verification.

For ocean systems, particular attention must be paid to:

- Local ecosystem changes
- Risk of red tides or harmful algal blooms
- Changes in nutrient balance
- Effects on currents and water temperature structure
- Coordination with fisheries, shipping, and coastal use
- Safety confirmation through long-term monitoring
- Consistency with international law and local regulations

For urban systems, the following must also be confirmed:

- Sanitation management
- Odor and pest control
- Safety of composting processes
- Effects on building loads
- Water quality management
- Consensus-building with local residents
- Maintenance costs

This plan does not recommend unverified large-scale deployment. Instead, it emphasizes gradual implementation based on publication, verification, improvement, and sharing.

## Open Invention Declaration

This plan treats the sustainability of the global environment as a common challenge for all humanity. It is not intended to be monopolized by any particular individual, company, or nation.

This technical philosophy and concept are released for the future of all life on Earth, based on natural laws and sustainable principles.

### Reasons for Opening

#### 1. Urgency

The global environmental crisis is not only a problem for future generations. It is a survival issue for humanity living today. Changes in climate, oceans, soil, water, and ecosystems involve time lags. If action begins only after the problem becomes fully obvious, it may be too late.

#### 2. Universality

Regeneration based on natural circulation is not limited to one region. Oceans, atmosphere, water, soil, and ecosystems are connected across national borders.

#### 3. Developability

By opening the concept, researchers, engineers, practitioners, and citizens worldwide can participate in improvement. Evolution through collective intelligence may proceed faster, more broadly, and more flexibly than closed development by a single inventor or company.

#### 4. Ethics

Technologies that maintain the global environment should be used not for exclusive profit, but for the future of life as a whole. Nature is not the property of anyone. It is the shared foundation of all life.
